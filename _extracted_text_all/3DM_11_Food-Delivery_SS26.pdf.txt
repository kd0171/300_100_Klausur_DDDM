       ▪ Platform-based business models act in a gig economy with volatile business partners

       ▪ Uber Eats introduces the three sided-market place

       ▪ Revenue mainly from restaurants (+user flat fee)

       ▪ Payments are made to delivery drivers

       ▪ Platform is not involved in physical transaction

       Properties
       ▪ The platform business model is applicable in different regions and scales in any order

       ▪ Decisions needed for the transactional control are taken in a fully automated fashion

       ▪ Partners in the three-sided market place occur and vanish at random

       ▪ The credibility of partners is maintained by monitoring, data analysis and ranking

       ▪ Data driven decision making is the key feature for running such a platform

       Incorporate multiple (contradicting) objectives
       ▪ Gross booking value is defined as the full amount of payments processed on the platform. It is
         achieved by placing pricy orders at popular restaurants and addresses restaurant earnings

       ▪ Marketplace fairness is in the interest of new or less popular restaurants. Earnings from such
         restaurants should be generated in order to prevent such restaurants from leaving the platform




                                                                                                                   https://www.adjust.com/glossary/conversion-rate/
       ▪ A conversion rate records the percentage of users who have completed a desired action. Here,
         its defines a user interaction that results in a meal order. This strengthen eater retention

       ▪ Gross booking value conflicts with marketplace fairness
       ▪ Gross booking value may conflict with conversion rate
       ▪ Conversion rate and marketplace fairness are independent
         of each other or may be even complementary

       Modeling (3)
       ▪ Parameter λ penalizes gross booking value that results from restaurant recommendations
          ▪ A small λ favors recommended restaurants and drives out low performing restaurants
          ▪ A high λ enforces that gross booking value is generated from low performers only
       ▪ Parameter α enforces a high conversion rate.
          ▪ Setting α ≈ 1 discharges promising solutions
          ▪ Setting α ≈ 0 allows for a poor conversion rate

       ▪ Both parameters have to be tuned online
       ▪ A multi-armed bandit maintains different parameter sets in parallel
       ▪ The framework controls whether to perform
         ▪ Exploitation (apply parameter sets that have shown to work well)
         ▪ Exploration (change parameter values in the hope of further improvement)                                https://yjjo.tistory.com/3

       ▪ A modified acquisition function suggests
