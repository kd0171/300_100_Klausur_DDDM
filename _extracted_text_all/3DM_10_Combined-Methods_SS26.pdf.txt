       Policy Classes I
       • Policy function approximation:
         Transfer a rule-of-thumb into a policy
       • Rolling-Horizon Re-Optimization:
         Determine decision based on current
         information (myopic)
       • Cost-Function Approximation:
         Manipulate reward function or constraints to
         incentivize (/avoid) (in)flexible decisions

       • None of the classes anticipates the future
         (information & decision model) explicitly

       Policy Classes II: Lookahead
       • Methods consider future changes via sampling of realizations
       • Rollout algorithm:
         • Simulate paths of sampled realizations and decisions made by a base policy
         • Use simulated values with Bellman Equation
       • Multiple Scenario Approach:
         • Sample sets of realizations (scenarios)
         • Solve scenarios individually
         • Determine most similar solution → decision

       • Disadvantages: runtime, decision making in simulation

       Disadvantages of ADP-Methods
       • Rolling horizon does not anticipate future developments
       • Policy function approximations do not exploit full detail of state and stochastic information
       • Cost function approximations are difficult to tune and ignore specifics in state and
         stochastic information
       • Lookahead methods usually require significant runtime and can only capture a few
         samples and decisions
       • Value function approximations require aggregation and storage of results, scalability
         issues
       • Further issues such as intuition, interpretability, etc.

       We can combine ADP-methods


       • Combinations of ADP-methods can exploit their strengths and alleviate their shortcomings

       • For example, integrate PFA, CFA, or VFA into a rollout algorithm

                • PFA or CFA can replace myopic base policy in rollout algorithm

                • Even VFA can evolve an improved base policy for rollout

                • The simulation horizon may be split into an 1. rollout and 2. VFA part

       Ad-hoc control of bike relocation
       •      Control of the decisions of a bike relocation truck

       •      Alternating decisions
               • At a station, decide on the number of bike to (un-)load
               • Once done (un-)loading, identify station with urgent need to go

       •      Urgent need is defined by missing bikes or missing bike racks

       •      Missing rack or missing bike define so called service fail

       •      One aims at the overall minimization of service fails over time

       •      In order to take decisions, a measure for the expected future fail of service is needed

       Problem Definition


       •      Station-based bike sharing system
       •      Time horizon
       •      Stations with capacity and initial fill levels
       •      Fleet of vehicles (1 in the simplest case)

       •      Stochastic customer trips (known probability)
       •      Dynamic routing and loading




       •      Objective: Minimize expected failed demands (pick up and drop off)

       Markov Decision Process
       •  Decision Point: Vehicle arrives at station
       •  States:
         • Vehicles (next stations and arrival times)
         • Stations (current fill levels)
       • Decisions:
         • Loading at current station
         • Next station to visit
       • Post-decision state: as state but with updated vehicle info and station fill level
       • Stochastic information: New demand for return and pickup
       • Transition:
         • New state when next vehicle arrives
         • Updated fill level based on demand
         • Observed failed demand

       Dynamic Lookahead Policy
       •     Loading:
            • Generate candidate solutions for current station (via
              PFA, 25%, 50%,75%)
            • Simulate future trips (customer demand) to evaluate
              candidate solutions
            • Select fill level with max prevented failed demand

       •     Routing:
            • Use same simulation to determine next station based on
                • Max prevented failed demand
                • Potential of other vehicles of the fleet
                   (assignment problem)

       Determine expected failed demand
       •      Consider to fill a station to 25% or 75%
       •      Simulation of future demand leads to
              failed requests for the 25% fill level
       •      Fill levels do not differ for the next 2½ hours

       •      Compare development of failed requests
              for three independent stations (n1, n2, n3)
       •      n3 receives one failed request early
       •      n2 receives increasing # of failed requests
       •      n3 receives many failed requests late in time
       •      On the long run the failed requests do not differ anymore

       •      Use VFA to learn hour-of-the-day dependent simulation horizons

       • Assume a policy 𝜋𝛿 with 𝛿 a tuning parameter

       Data Driven Decision Making (theory)
       •  Data becomes important if
         • it changes over time and requires a temporal consideration
         • changes are stochastic or even distributions are unknown
         • variations are so large that information gain becomes important
       • Dynamic decision making comes into play
         • an external stochastic process has to be modeled
         • an internal decision process has to be modeled
         • Both are tied by succeeding state representations
         • Markov process sequentially models stepwise
       • Policy making by
         • Policy function approx., cost function approx., look ahead, value func. approx.
         • There is no favorite technique, application relates to curses of dimensionality

       Data Driven Decision Making (application)
       •  A priori decision making has its advantages
         • Optimality may be warranted
         • Setup time for implementation
         • Communication of solution to stakeholders
       • Dynamic decision making in the face of significant information gain
         • Assignment of internal resources to external demand
         • Current decisions impact future flexibility
         • Anticipation incorporates expected future reward
       • Decision making combines
         • Optimization (first term of Bellman equation)
         • Analytics (second term of Bellman equation)
         • Combination of searching for unknown features and conclusion of statistical correlation
