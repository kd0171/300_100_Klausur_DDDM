  • If measure is below threshold, select A, else, select B

   value-function
• No explicit anticipation
• In some cases: PFA is the result of analytical

Summary
•    No anticipation of future changes in information and decisions
•    No flexibility of solution
•    Suitable for problems with mild impact of stochasticity and dynamism
•    Problems where feasible decisions are already hard to find (NP-hard decision space)
•    Does not work if future reward is large compared to immediate reward
•    Examples:
    • Works acceptably well for scheduling with stochastic set-up times and many additional
      constraints
    • Works not at all for courier services (no flexibility for new requests, no coverage of
      service area) or inventory management (no refilling)

Cost Function Approximation
• Idea:
  • Incentivize (penalize) selection of “good” (“bad”) decisions by modification of reward term
  • Prohibit bad decisions by manipulating constraints
• 𝑥 = argmin𝑥∈𝑋 𝑆 (𝑅 𝑆, 𝑥 + 𝐼(𝑆, 𝑥))
• 𝐴+Θ 𝑥 ≤ 𝑏+𝜃

• 𝐼 depends on state and decision (can be negative)
• Some parallels to Bellman Equation (Powell 2011 denotes 𝐼 as 𝑉)

• Θ, 𝜃 are usually globally set, but are also varied recently (e.g., time-dependent)
• Feasibility has to be ensured when selecting Θ, 𝜃
• No explicit anticipation of stochasticity and future decision making.
